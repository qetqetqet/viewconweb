Good news for creative professionals who’ve wished they could connect a PowerBook G4 to one of Apple’s gorgeous flat-panel displays: Now you can.

Need to add an external display for a presentation or to increase your workspace back at the office? In addition to featuring DVI, VGA, S-Video and composite output options for connecting to CRTs, TVs and projectors, the PowerBook G4 can also connect to Apple’s gorgeous flat-panel displays. All you need is the new 667MHz or 800MHz PowerBook G4 and optional Pro Video Apple DVI to ADC Adapter, and you can connect to the 23-inch Apple Cinema HD Display, the 22-inch Apple Cinema Display, or the 17-inch or 15-inch Apple Studio Displays.

The Apple DVI to ADC Adapter offers more than just a physical connection between a DVI port and a digital ADC monitor. It’s been designed to maintain the digital integrity of the video signal to give you the ultimate pure digital experience.

The adapter provides an all-digital signal path between your computer and the display. It features active components that regenerate both the digital graphics signal and the USB signal coming from the computer. This regeneration helps ensure the highest quality of the displayed image even at the extremely fast signal rates used by the Apple Cinema HD Display.

A compact design enables the Apple DVI to ADC Adapter to fit easily on or under your desk. The Apple DVI to ADC Adapter comes with a power supply, power cord and DVI/USB cable.

A generational leap in performance
The PowerBook G4’s generational leap in performance is most visible in the realm of 3D graphics. That’s due to a combination of factors, chief among them being the powerful ATI Mobility Radeon 7500 graphics processing unit (GPU), Mac OS X, OpenGL, a 15.2-inch megawide display, and — not least — the way they all combine to create eye-popping effects in millions of colors.

Sizzling graphics processor
The PowerBook G4 comes loaded with the most powerful graphics processor that’s ever been shipped with an Apple notebook computer. It’s called the ATI Mobility Radeon 7500, and it provides unparalleled 2D, 3D and multimedia graphics performance for today’s most demanding applications — in full 32-bit color.

Fact is, the Mobility Radeon 7500 performs astonishingly fast 32-bit true color rendering. It supports multi-texturing and new 3D effects without compromising speed. Full transformation, clipping and lighting is hardware accelerated, bringing 3D worlds to life with incredible detail and photorealism — at phenomenal performance levels. Just wait till you see how realistically the Mobility Radeon 7500 renders such elements as metals, liquids and wood — as well as their interactions with lights and shadows.

With 32 megabytes of onboard double data rate (DDR) memory at its disposal, the ATI Mobility Radeon 7500 graphics processor with AGP 4x support enables graphics applications to treat main memory as a seamless extension of the graphics processor. That way, large visual elements can refresh themselves swiftly after image manipulations like filters, blurs and color corrections.

Using its advanced AGP 4X architecture (perfect for accelerating transfers of textures and data) and internal graphics engine processing digital data at 230MHz, the PowerBook G4 tears through 3D effects, making it the dream machine for professionals who like to carry their creative tools with them wherever they go.

Take this simple test
There’s a quick way to assess a computer’s graphics capability, and that’s by taking it out for a spin on a graphics-intensive 3D game. That’s because 3D games demand fast frame rates, and — since each frame of gaming action must be redrawn as quickly as possible — routinely push the graphics processor to its limits. The frame rate (or how quickly each individual frame of game play is redrawn) is critically important since a faster frame rate means more frames are being generated to create a smoother, movie-like effect.

Take Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos, Blizzard Entertainment’s hot new title coming to the Macintosh platform. Warcraft III features full multiplayer support — including shared unit control and resource trading between players — and Internet play over Battle.net, with sizzling 3D graphics powered by Blizzard’s famed graphics engine. You’ll encounter the savage, green-skinned Orcs, the reclusive Night Elves, and the horrifying army known as the Scourge. Your mission is to build legendary heroes to combat these menaces and advance in levels, armed only with your finely-honed reflexes and your PowerBook G4.

Get the big picture
Want to show your stuff on the big screen? Your new PowerBook G4’s DVI port can detect when it’s connected to an external display or projector. The system automatically displays to the external monitor when you connect it to the PowerBook. Alternatively, you can toggle between dual display and video mirroring modes through one touch of the F7 key. And here’s the best part: The hardware mirroring included in the new PowerBook G4 allows video content such as QuickTime movies to be displayed on an external monitor with performance equal to that of the built-in display.

Picture this
You can use the PowerBook G4’s S-video output port for connecting to an analog TV. And thanks to the built-in Combo (DVD-ROM/CD-RW) drive, it gets even better when you’re in the mood to watch movies instead of making them.


The right connections
The PowerBook G4 features DVI, VGA, S-video, and composite output options for connecting to CRTs, TVs, and projectors. It can also connect to Apple’s gorgeous flat-panel displays.

OpenGL runs even faster
The ATI Mobility Radeon 7500 takes full advantage of OpenGL, an advanced 3D technology — which now runs even faster under Mac OS X.

DVD video playback
Incidentally, the ATI Mobility Radeon 7500 graphics processor also helps ensure full-frame-rate DVD playback. This is a vital asset when you’re traveling on business: with a brilliant 15.2-inch display at your disposal, you’ll be taking your own private screening room with you. The Combo (DVD-ROM/CD-RW) drive that comes standard with the new PowerBook G4 systems plays DVD movies, plus DVD-ROM discs and all your music CDs — and lets you burn music and data CDs.

The Quake test
3D games push the graphics processing unit harder than any other application. And of these 3D games, Quake performance has come to be the benchmark against which all graphics processors are measured. So you’ll be pleased to hear that when playing the Quake III Arena version 1.30, in millions of colors, at 1024x768 resolution, the 800MHz PowerBook G4 blazes away at a scorching 68 frames per second.*

 * Higher frame rates indicate better performance. Tests conducted by Apple.
